What Does a Counselling Psychologist Do?

Counselling psychologists provide therapy and support to individuals struggling with mental health challenges.

Provides Therapy

Offers psychological support and therapy sessions to improve mental well-being.

Conducts Assessments

Evaluates emotional and behavioral issues using various techniques.

Offers Guidance

Helps clients cope with stress, trauma, and life challenges.

When Should You Reach Out?

If you experience any of these challenges, seeking help can make a difference.

  • Struggling with stress or anxiety
  • Issues in relationships or family
  • Coping with loss or trauma
  • Feeling overwhelmed or depressed
Common Mental Health Myths Busted

Myth: Mental health problems are rare.

Fact: Mental health problems affect millions of people worldwide and can affect anyone, regardless of age or background.

Myth: Therapy is only for people with serious problems.

Fact: Therapy is for anyone looking to improve their mental well-being and develop better coping skills.

Myth: Talking about your feelings makes them worse.

Fact: Talking about feelings can help release built-up emotions and provide a sense of relief.

Quick Mental Wellness Tips

Practice Deep Breathing

Take a moment to practice deep breathing when you feel overwhelmed. This can help calm your mind and body.

Stay Physically Active

Physical activity is great for mental health. A daily walk or exercise routine can boost your mood and reduce anxiety.

Set Boundaries

Learning to say no is crucial for maintaining healthy boundaries. Don’t overextend yourself to avoid burnout.

Frequently Asked Questions

What can I expect during my first session?

Your first session will be an opportunity to discuss your goals, concerns, and background. It will set the foundation for future sessions.

Do I need to prepare anything before my session?

No preparation is required. Simply come with an open mind, and we will discuss what's on your mind.

Is counselling confidential?

Yes, everything discussed in therapy is confidential unless there is a risk of harm to you or others.
